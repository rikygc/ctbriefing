I
The Eternal War Is Over

CT was built to eradicate cross-teaming in NBTF. We did it so effectively, so visibly, and so persistently that the game itself was forced to adopt our mission as official policy. The Eternal War ended not in defeat — but in total victory.

Most factions dissolve because they fail. CT is still standing because it won. The crossteaming report command — the heartbeat of CT's original mission — will be preserved as a legacy system. The historic score of the Eternal War remains viewable as a permanent monument to what CT accomplished. It still functions if NBTF moderation is unavailable. But the war it was built to fight is over.

The Legacy Pillar

The Eternal War is CT's founding monument. Every new member will know where CT came from and what it accomplished. That history never disappears — it becomes the bedrock everything new is built on.

With no external enemy left, CT's two companies — Ravens and Enforcers — faced a choice. Dissolve. Drift. Or turn to face each other. They chose to face each other.

The Inner War is not chaos. It is not the faction falling apart. It is organized, honorable, and endless conflict — two armies bound by the same history and the same Eternal War they won together, now locked in perpetual competition for dominance. The War Council was formed to ensure the conflict stays honorable. The Inner War has begun.

This Is A Proposal

This document requires a majority council vote to become official CT policy. It cannot be enacted unilaterally. If passed, it triggers a full Faction-Wide Redevelopment Program — every existing CT system reviewed and amended to fit the new mission and lore. Nothing changes overnight. Everything gets evaluated deliberately and collectively.

IV
Public Systems

CT's power was always its systems. The Inner War introduces public-facing mechanics that plug regular NBTF players into CT's conflict — making CT visible, relevant, and worth joining for people who aren't members yet.

System 01
For-Hire
Public Reinforcement

Any NBTF player can request backup mid-game through CTBOT. A command pings available Ravens or Enforcers based on which side needs help. Assist succeeds — core blown, core saved, objective completed — the assisting CT member earns CTC. Low commitment, high impact. Plugs the public into CT's conflict without requiring them to join.

System 02
Hitman
Contract Kills

Any NBTF player can place a hit on a specific target through CTBOT. Submit the target's name and a CTC bounty. CT accepts the contract. A Raven or Enforcer hunts the target in-game and submits proof of elimination. Bounty paid out on confirmed kill. Immersive, addictive, and creates ongoing engagement between CT and the broader server.

System 03
War Bank
Central CTC Treasury

One central CTC pool governed neutrally by the War Council. Both companies draw from it for for-hire payouts, hitman contracts, and event rewards. Neither company can access it unilaterally. Winning official war events earns the victorious company a larger share of that cycle's CTC distribution — merit-based stakes.

System 04
War Standings
Season Score Tracking

Raven vs Enforcer running score across all official engagements. Updated publicly after every official war event. Season-based resets with records preserved. Visible to the whole server. The standings are CT's live scoreboard — and the primary measure of which company currently holds dominance.

The External Threat Clause
When The Inner War Pauses

When the War Council unanimously determines that an external force poses a significant threat to CT, its members, or NBTF stability — the Inner War is immediately suspended. Both companies fall under unified command for the duration of the external conflict.

Unanimous War Council vote required to declare an external threat
Threat must be documented and presented to both Commanders before the vote
Both Commanders can contest the declaration but cannot veto it
Suspension reviewed every two weeks — council must reconfirm or lift
War standings frozen during suspension — resume exactly where they left off
Unanimous vote required to lift suspension and resume the Inner War

In the Inner War, Ravens and Enforcers are enemies. But they are CT enemies — bound by the same history and the same Eternal War they won together. When something outside threatens what they built, the old alliance surfaces without hesitation. Not because they like each other. Because no outsider gets to touch what's theirs.

Every defection attempt is a public event — the whole server can watch. The defector who makes it becomes legend. The one who gets caught becomes a cautionary tale. Defection trials are CT's most dramatic recurring content and its most powerful recruitment material.

VI
The Event Structure

The Inner War runs on events. Existing CT events will be retrofitted to fit the new identity and mission — reframed around the Raven vs Enforcer conflict and Inner War lore. New events specific to the Inner War will be developed as part of the Redevelopment Program. Together they create a full calendar that keeps both companies active, competitive, and engaged.

OW
Official War Events
The centerpiece of the Inner War. Scheduled and announced server-wide. Full Raven vs Enforcer battle with a count system — kills, objectives, core outcomes tracked. Pre-battle lore declarations, post-battle reports in character. Winner takes the round — standings updated publicly. These are CT's biggest moments and the primary driver of the season score.
PB
Practice Battles
Raven vs Enforcer — smaller, unranked skirmishes between official events. No standings impact. Tactics developed, new members get experience, the rivalry stays hot between scheduled events. Any Pacesetter can call a practice battle at their discretion.
IS
Intra-Squad Battles
Same-team competition — Ravens vs Ravens, Enforcers vs Enforcers. Determines internal rankings and identifies top performers for official war events. Builds squad-level chemistry within each company. No cross-company stakes but significant internal implications for who gets deployed in the big events.
DT
Defection Trials
Public events open to the whole server as spectators. The Hunt and The Sponsored Crossing are CT's most dramatic recurring content. High-rank defection attempts — the politics of sponsorship, the tension of the crossing, the full company hunt — draw the entire server's attention. Win or lose, every trial feeds the lore and the legend.
TN
Tournaments
Structured competitive events — 1v1s, squad vs squad within the same company, cross-company brackets. Season-based with standings implications. Give individual members a path to personal recognition beyond company affiliation. The best fighters from both sides compete for individual legacy.
Redevelopment Note

All existing CT events will be reviewed as part of the Redevelopment Program. Those that fit the Inner War identity get retrofitted with new lore framing and updated scoring. Those that don't get archived respectfully alongside the Eternal War records. New events specific to the Inner War era will be developed collaboratively by both companies.

VII
RP, Lore & Storytelling

The Inner War isn't just an operational shift. It comes with a living narrative — and that narrative is one of CT's most powerful new assets. RP and storytelling are no longer background flavor. In this era, they are front-facing and pivotal to everything CT does.

01
Lore as Infrastructure

The narrative around the Eternal War ending and the Inner War beginning isn't decoration — it's the backbone of CT's new identity. Every event, every defection trial, every season result feeds into a living story. The lore is what makes a core explosion feel like a chapter in something bigger than a single match.

02
A New Recruitment Angle

CT never had a home for players drawn to serious RP, faction lore, and character-driven gameplay. The Inner War creates one. People who want immersion — not just PvP — now have a reason to join. The ongoing narrative gives them material to engage with and a reason to stay beyond any single event.

03
Seasonal Story Arcs

Each season of the Inner War has a narrative arc. Standings aren't just numbers — they're the story of which company dominated that chapter of CT's history. Season endings get documented. Upsets get remembered. The faction's history writes itself through competition, and the server follows it like an ongoing series.

04
War Declarations & Ceremony

Every official war event opens with a formal declaration — written in character, posted publicly before the battle begins. Each company makes their statement. After the event, the result is announced with the same weight. Defection trials have their own ceremony language. The Inner War always feels like it matters because it always sounds like it matters.

Stability Through Story

Factions with active lore and narrative culture retain members longer than those built purely on PvP activity. When the server is quiet, the story keeps people connected. When someone returns after inactivity, the narrative gives them something to catch up on — a reason to re-engage. The Inner War's story is CT's longest running event.

VIII
The Recruitment Trial

Joining CT in the Inner War era isn't just signing up. Before a recruit chooses their company, they go through three mandatory experiences designed to give them a real feel for both sides. The choice they make at the end is informed — not a guess based on a name or aesthetic.

I
The Raven Mission
The recruit accompanies Raven Company on an actual operation. No obligation, no pressure. They see how Ravens move, think, communicate, and fight — intelligence-focused, precision-oriented, facility-sided. One real mission. Not a demo, not a tour.
II
The Enforcer Mission
The recruit accompanies Enforcer Company on an actual operation. Same format — real mission, real combat, real team dynamic. Brute force, aggression, rebel-sided. The recruit experiences the full contrast between the two companies firsthand before making any decision.
III
The Deciding Battle
The recruit chooses a side for one live Inner War battle — no consequences for this choice. It is a trial run. They fight alongside their chosen company against the other in a real engagement. At the end they make their final allegiance declaration. Whatever they choose in Event III becomes their company — until they use their one lifetime defection.
Why This Works

Recruits make an informed decision based on real experience — not vibes. It also plugs new members into active events before they've officially joined a company. Their first three CT experiences are actual combat. That's a better introduction than any document or welcome message.

OPERATION
Culmination
If CT ends — this is how

This section exists because the council deserves the full picture. The Inner War is CT's best shot at a new era of real, sustainable identity. But if the proposal fails to pass — or if after a defined runway it has not taken hold — the council faces one final choice.

Operation Culmination is that choice. It is not a failure. It is not a retreat. It is CT deciding, on its own terms, that the mission is complete.

CT was built to eradicate cross-teaming in NBTF. We did it so completely that the game adopted our mission as official policy. The war is over. We won. Operation Culmination is how we declare that — loudly, proudly, and in front of the entire server — before CT fades into silence on its own.

Most factions die quietly. Members drift, leadership goes inactive, the server sits empty. That is not how CT ends. If CT ends, it ends the way it lived — with presence, with intention, and with the whole server watching.

What Operation Culmination Looks Like
I
The Declaration
CEO Potato makes the official announcement to the faction and to NBTF at large. CT formally declares the Eternal War won and the mission complete. Every member, every founder, every era credited. Published everywhere CT has a presence.
II
The Archive
Every CT document, system, guide, and piece of history formally archived and preserved. The Eternal War record, the bylaws, the org chart, the event logs — all documented. CT's legacy does not disappear. It becomes a record of what a faction built right looks like.
III
The Final Event
CT hosts one final server-wide event in NBTF — open to everyone. A celebration of everything the faction stood for. Founders recognized. Veterans honored. The whole server invited to play alongside CT one last time. No enforcement. No ops. Just CT being CT — together, one final time.
IV
The Ceremony
Formal in-Discord closing ceremony. Founder acknowledgments. A reading of CT's history and achievements. Every member who contributed recognized by name. The faction officially disbanded with full ceremony — not deleted, not abandoned. Closed.
V
The Legacy
The CT Discord remains open permanently as a read-only archive. The history, the documents, the screenshots, the Eternal War record — all preserved. Anyone who was ever part of CT can return and see what was built. The server stays. The faction ends. The legacy doesn't.
