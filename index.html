I
The Honest Situation

CT has been in a holding pattern for about a month. That's on me. I've been developing the vision for what comes next without transferring it to the people who need to execute it. This document ends that.

NBTF's decision to officially address cross-teaming was a direct hit to CT's core identity. The problem we were built to solve is now being handled by the game itself. Our enforcement era — Phase I and Phase II of CT's existence — is over.

Note

That is not a failure. That is a completion. CT operated so visibly and so persistently that the game itself was forced to act. Most factions dissolve because they fail. We are still here because we won.

But winning the original mission while standing still is how factions die slowly. The faction ecosystem in NBTF is collapsing. Unofficial groups are absorbing players because they demand nothing — no structure, no accountability, no real community. Official factions are dying one by one. HF is the only one still standing with real presence. We are the other one — barely.

New players enter NBTF every day with no guidance. No one walks them through the map. No one explains the mechanics. No one gives them a real community before the chaos does. That is the gap CT is uniquely positioned to fill — and we already built the systems to do it.

CTBC, CTWC, and CTWG have existed in various stages of development for months. We built them thinking they were supplementary to enforcement. They were actually the foundation of what comes next. Phase III activates what we already built.

II
The New Mission

The old mission was one sentence: eradicate cross-teaming in NBTF. Clear, urgent, and actionable. The new mission needs the same clarity.

CT Mission Statement — Phase III
CT exists to welcome every new player into NBTF, give them the tools to thrive, and show them what a real faction looks like.

This is not a downgrade. While everything else in NBTF's faction ecosystem breaks down, CT becomes the standard — the last official faction with real structure, real progression, and real culture.

We are not fixing something as immediately visible as cross-teaming. We are fixing something slower and more fundamental: new players have nowhere real to land when they enter this game. CT becomes that place.

Legacy Advantage

No new faction can walk into NBTF with 250+ members, server-wide name recognition, and the infrastructure we have. That advantage only exists if we exploit it deliberately and immediately. New factions wish they were starting where we are. Use it.

Think of Phase III as a new faction that happens to carry a legacy. Same name, same people, same history — but a fundamentally different identity, mission, and operational structure. Approach this with the energy of a launch, not a renovation.

III
The Three Pillars

Everything CT does from this point forward runs through three systems. They are not separate programs. They are one connected pipeline — each one feeding into the next.

Pillar 01
CTWG
The Street Team

On-call casual presence on NBTF servers. Members squad up, play together, help new players they encounter, and keep CT visible and approachable. No patrol structure. No enforcement. Presence first — branding and recruitment follow naturally.

Pillar 02
CTWC
The Front Door

Internal onboarding for every new CT member before anything else. Tour Guides walk them through NBTF — map, mechanics, weapons, vehicles, and core systems. Warm, personal, low-pressure. Closes with a direct handoff to CTBC.

Pillar 03
CTBC
The Core

Bootcamp is now CT's primary activity and main identity. Three modules, structured progression, graduation ceremony. This is where members are made. Where Raven and Enforcer come from. Where CT's culture gets transmitted.

VI
The 8-Week Runway

Phase III is not open-ended. CT operates on a defined runway. At the end of 8 weeks the council evaluates honestly — is the pipeline working, is activity recovering, are members engaged. If yes, CT found its new identity. If no, CT closes deliberately and with dignity.

Either outcome is handled with intention. CT does not fade. We choose how this ends.

Week 1–2 — Foundation
Council Alignment & System Activation
Council reads and aligns on this briefing. CEO delivers faction-wide announcement. COO briefs the Pacesetter team. HR begins role description updates. CTWG relaunch communicated to Watch Commanders. CTWC Tour Guide pipeline activated. Server channels updated.
Week 3–4 — First Cycle
CTBC Cycle One Launches
First official CTBC cycle begins. New members routed through CTWC before entering bootcamp. CTWG sessions running regularly. Pacesetters operating under updated quotas. Bylaws amendment submitted for council vote.
Week 5–6 — Evaluation
Mid-Point Check
LPCS submits mid-cycle progress report. Council reviews pipeline flow, activity levels, member engagement, and recruitment numbers. Adjustments made based on real data — not assumptions. No major structural changes at this stage.
Week 7–8 — Decision
First Graduation & Final Assessment
First CTBC graduation ceremony. Council holds full assessment — is the identity working? Is the pipeline self-sustaining? Decision on CT's future made on real data. If the pivot succeeds, CT continues. If not, Operation Culmination is called to a vote.
VII
From CoS to Council

I want to be direct about something before I close this out.

I am CoS. I am not CEO. I am not Chairman. The ideas in this document are mine — developed over the past month because someone had to think about where CT goes from here. But developing the idea and executing the idea are two very different things, and execution is not my job alone.

Potato — this is your faction to lead. Systems — this is your operation to run. The council as a whole owns this transition. I can support, guide, and consult — but I cannot and should not be carrying this across the finish line. That dynamic is part of what stalled us to begin with.

Important

Everything in this briefing is a proposal, not a decree. These are the ideas I believe give CT the best shot at a successful pivot — but I don't have all the answers and I'm not in the room the way you are. If something doesn't work, adjust it. If you see a better approach, take it. What matters is that the council is aligned on the mission and moving together.

This is a hands-on project that requires insight, contribution, and ownership from everyone on this council. I'm asking you to treat it that way.

CT survived leadership changes, inactivity cycles, and a war. It'll survive this too — because we're choosing to evolve instead of waiting to collapse. The pipeline is built. The systems exist. The brand still carries weight. Everything we need to succeed in Phase III we already have. The only thing missing is the council deciding to move.

Presence first.
People second.
Everything else follows.

OPERATION
Culmination
If the pivot fails — this is how CT ends

This section exists because the council deserves to know the full picture. Phase III is CT's best shot at a new era. But if the 8-week runway ends and the pivot has not taken hold — activity has not recovered, the pipeline is not functioning, and leadership is still waiting on one person to keep things moving — then the council has a choice to make.

Operation Culmination is that choice. It is not a failure. It is not a retreat. It is CT deciding, on its own terms, that the mission is complete.

CT was built to eradicate cross-teaming in NBTF. We did it so effectively that the game adopted our mission as official policy. The war is over. We won. Operation Culmination is how we declare that — loudly, proudly, and on our own terms — before the faction fades into silence.

Most factions die quietly. Members drift away, leadership goes inactive, and eventually the server just sits empty. That is not how CT ends. If CT ends, it ends the way it lived — with presence, with intention, and with the entire server watching.

What Operation Culmination Looks Like
I
The Declaration
CEO Potato makes the official announcement to the faction and to NBTF at large. CT formally declares the war against cross-teaming won. The announcement credits every member, every founder, every era of CT's history. This is published everywhere — Discord, NBTF, any public CT presence.
II
The Archive
Every CT document, system, guide, and piece of history is formally archived and preserved. CTBC, CTWC, CTWG, the bylaws, the org chart, the event logs — all of it documented and stored. CT's legacy does not disappear. It becomes a record of what a faction built right looks like.
III
The Final Event
CT hosts one final server-wide event in NBTF — open to everyone, not just CT members. A celebration of everything the faction stood for. Founders recognized. Veteran members honored. The whole server invited to play alongside CT one last time. No enforcement, no ops. Just CT being CT — together, one final time.
IV
The Ceremony
Following the final event, a formal in-Discord closing ceremony. Founder acknowledgments. A reading of CT's history and achievements. Every member who contributed recognized by name. The faction officially disbanded with full ceremony — not deleted, not abandoned. Closed.
V
The Legacy
The CT Discord server remains open as a read-only archive. The history, the documents, the screenshots — all preserved. Anyone who was ever part of CT can return and see what was built. The server stays. The faction ends. The legacy doesn't.
